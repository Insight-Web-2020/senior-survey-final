Academics

Indian Institute Of Technology Bombay, being the most esteemed engineering institute of India, has seen many facets of students when it comes to academics. We get to see many aspects and attitudes of students in general towards academics at IIT Bombay.

We surveyed the passing out batch to get a more thorough insight into students mentality towards the academic environment here at the institute.

Here is an overview of the same.

  • A total of 215 students filled the survey form and presented their views across a wide range of questions ranging from departments to CPI to attitude towards academics and many more.
  • From the total of 215 students, 211 students are graduating this year while the remaining 4 students took extensions.
  • Constantly recognized as the most decorated institute of the country, 95.2% of students agreed that CPI plays a very important role.
  • Upon asking, around 66% students said that they were highly enthusiastic about the core sector of their department, which positively showed that they liked their department.

Department

Which Department you are from?

This analysis shows that the awareness of the form was distributed among all the graduating students. The majority of responses came from CS and Chemical Engineering department, followed by Electrical Engineering and Mechanical Engineering.



What Degree are you Graduating With?

From the corresponding graduating students, a majority of them graduated with a B.Tech degree. Additionally, 13% and 4% students graduated with Honors and Minors degree respectively.

Among others, 13% were Dual Degree holders with additional 6% with a Minors and Honors degree too. The remaining 2% were B.S certificate holders.



How different was the reality compared to your expectations of your Department (before coming to IIT)?

Upon asking this question, 30.33% answered affirmatively to the question, saying, “It was better than expected.” On a contrary, 29.85% said, “It was disappointing.” While the rest 39.81% were happy with what they received.



CPI

What was your CPI?

The above graph clearly shows that a vast majority of students(79) were in between 8-9 CPI group. The density around 8-9 is far more greater, followed by the 7-8 CPI group with a total 54 students. Additional 39 students were in the CPI range of 9-10.



How important a role do you think one’s CPI plays?

Gauging the responses of the students, most of them told that CPI does play an important role and one should maintain a good level of CPI. 73.4% of the students rated the importance of CPI, 4 and above. 21.8% rated it 3 and the rest 4.8% rated it below 3.



Interests

Were you able to gauge your academic interests?

While 57.8% of students were able to gauge their academic interests, the rest either got some clarity or were still confused.



Were you interested in core?

Upon asking their interests in Core, the plot shows that around 139 students out of 215 students were initially interested in Core Sector, which reduced to 112 at the end.

Similarly, the rest of the students showed no interested in the core sector when they joined the institute, which got further lengthened to 45.5% from 34.1%.



When did you lose/gain interest in core?

With the follow up to the previous question, 47 students of the following 215 students lost interest in core, while on the other hand, 24 others gained interest in the core sector.

While most of them said their interest remained the same throughout the period, 23% of them stated they lost interest in their 2nd year and 3rd year both. Followed by this, 8% lost their interest in their 1st year only, 6% in their 4th year and the rest 0.5% in their 5th year(for DD).



Attitude

What was your general attitude towards your academic performance?

Out of 215 students, 37.9% admitted that they were sincere but failed to reach their potential. 34.1% just wanted a decent CPI, the other 30 students just wanted to sail through. And the remaining rest 29 students agreed upon academics being their first priority throughout and were sincere.



How regularly did you study?

About 65% of students studied just before quizzes and 21% of students studied just before the midsems/endsems. Very few students stayed ahead of the class.



How competitive do you think the environment is here?

Most of the students believe that the environment was farely competitive.



Attendance

On average, how many classes do you think you have attended?

Highest 74 students attended 60-80% of the classes while only 10 students attended 0-20% of lectures



What were your reasons for not attending classes?

Total 178 students stated that attending classes was not beneficial for them, 83 students stated that they didn't attended classes because of laziness, while the remaining students opted the other options.



Had you been given an option to not attend any class, how regularly would you have attended?

About 50% of students would have still regularly attended the lectures while the rest would have either never attended the lectures or rarely or just before exams.



Teaching

How involving were the teaching methodologies adopted in the Institute?

The rating above shows that most of the students were not happy with the teaching methodologies in the Institute. Only 20.4% students rated the teaching methods in the institute as good and very good. A majority of them rated the teaching methods in the institute as average with a rating of 45.5%.



How effective do you think going to classes is?

Most of the students think that attending classes was effective.



How would you rate the institute on the grounds of academic flexibility? (in terms of Minors, Credit System, ALC etc.)

About 68% of students found the system to be to very flexible, while others had some issues with it.



Honesty

How honest were you with your academics?

Highest 121 students stated that they were totally honest with their academics throughout while only 4 students stated that they were dishonest.



What propelled you to use unfair means?

No interest in the course was the most common reason that propelled students to use unfair means.



In which of the following did you indulge in unfair means?

Almost half of the students said that they never cheated, and in the other half, most students resorted to unfair means in assignments and homework.
